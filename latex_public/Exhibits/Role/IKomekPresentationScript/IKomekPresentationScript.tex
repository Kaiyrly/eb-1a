\subsubsection{iKOMEK109 — Presentation script (speaker notes)}
\label{subsubsec:IKomekPresentationScript}

\paragraph{Document identity.}
Speaker notes that accompany the iKOMEK 2.0 slide deck. The script explains each dashboard, process block, and KPI and how they connect to city outcomes.

\paragraph{Key passages (excerpts).}
\begin{itemize}\small
  \item \textbf{Executive cadence:} how weekly analytics for the Mayor prioritize urgent issues and compress cycle times.
  \item \textbf{Backlog program:} root-cause categories, SLA tiers, automated escalations, and throughput tracking.
  \item \textbf{Channels:} product decisions behind the Telegram bot and mobile app and their effect on adoption (\emph{+22\%}).
  \item \textbf{Safety chain:} case routing from detection to field services and linkage to enforcement outcomes and fines collected.
  \item \textbf{Election integrity:} the data quality steps (address normalization, geocoding, deduplication) needed to reach \emph{100\%}.
  \item \textbf{Roadmap:} CRM architecture, API catalog, executive rating tools, DB optimization, and SSO strategy.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Relevance to USCIS Criterion 8.}
The narrative shows \emph{how} Mr.\ Taximov led complex municipal IT and analytics, translating architecture and process into measurable results—precisely the “detailed and probative” account USCIS expects.
